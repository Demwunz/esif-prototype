Priority axis

A priority axis is an aim the government has to fix a problem.

  • For example, one priority axis is to reduce youth unemployment.
  • We publish the priorities on GOV.UK, explaining why they are important.
  • We review and change the priorities regularly to make sure the most urgent problems are solved.
  • Charities, local government and businesses design programmes to solve these problems.
    • We look at how the programmes perform to find out:
  • what has been successful (so we can repeat that success for other priorities)
  • what problems have been solved (so we can adjust the priority axis)

  • You can read about priority axes [INSERT LINK TO INFO] on GOV.UK
  • Tell us which axis your programme will target.

Investment priority

Priority axes are grouped into subjects, for example 'reduce youth unemployment'.

In the priority axis for reducing youth unemployment, there are different investment priorities.

These investment priorities take a closer look at the axis and target specific problems.

You can find your investment priority on the GOV.UK priority axis list [INSERT LINK].
